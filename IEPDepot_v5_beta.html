Privacy Policy

How IEP Depot™ handles your information

Overview

IEP Depot™ is a browser-based IEP progress monitoring tool developed by IEP Depot™. This Privacy Policy explains how IEP Depot™ handles information entered during use. IEP Depot™ is designed from the ground up to protect student privacy and comply with FERPA (Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act).

Effective Date: 2026  |  Contact: hello@iepdepot.com

What Data IEP Depot™ Collects

IEP Depot™ collects only the information you manually enter. All data is stored in your browser's session storage on your device only.

  • Data is stored locally on your device, not on any external server
  • Data clears automatically when you close the browser tab
  • No data is transmitted to IEP Depot™ or any third party
  • No tracking pixels, cookies, or analytics are used
  • No account or login is required

FERPA Compliance

IEP Depot™ is specifically designed to eliminate FERPA exposure at the platform level through a student code system:

  • No student legal names are stored. Teachers assign each student a private code (e.g., S01, S02). Only the teacher knows which code belongs to which student.
  • The code-to-student mapping is never entered into IEP Depot™. It is kept privately by the teacher.
  • IEP Depot™ does not constitute an "education record" under FERPA because it contains no personally identifiable student information.
  • Disability categories, grade bands, and goal data are attached to codes only.

Data Storage and Security

  • All data is stored in browser sessionStorage — temporary local storage that exists only while the tab is open
  • sessionStorage is sandboxed per tab — other tabs and websites cannot access it
  • No data leaves your device. IEP Depot™ has no server, no database, and no internet requirement after initial page load
  • Closing the browser tab permanently clears all data
  • IEP Depot™ does not use cookies, localStorage, IndexedDB, or any other persistent browser storage

Parent and Student Information

  • IEP Depot™ has no parent portal. Progress reports are printed and mailed to parents.
  • IEP Depot™ does not assume parents have internet access.
  • No parent or student contact information is collected or stored by IEP Depot™.
  • Students over age 18 retain their own FERPA rights. Follow your district's policies for sharing IEP information with adult students.

Teacher and School Information

IEP Depot™ does not collect, store, or transmit teacher names, email addresses, credentials, school names, district names, or building information. Staff identifiers entered (e.g., "T-Smith") are stored in sessionStorage only and are never transmitted externally.

Third-Party Services

IEP Depot™ loads zero external resources. All fonts, styles, and scripts are fully self-contained within this file. No data of any kind is sent to any third party. No analytics, advertising networks, tracking pixels, or external APIs are used. IEP Depot™ makes no network calls after the initial file load.

ADA / WCAG 2.1 AA Accessibility

  • Minimum 4.5:1 color contrast ratio for all body text; 3:1 for large text and UI components
  • All interactive elements meet the 44×44px minimum touch target size
  • Visible focus indicators on all keyboard-navigable elements
  • ARIA labels on all icon-only buttons and non-descriptive controls
  • Semantic HTML with proper heading hierarchy (h1–h3)
  • Skip-to-main-content link for keyboard users
  • Reduced motion support via CSS prefers-reduced-motion media query
  • All form fields have explicit, programmatically associated labels
  • Color is never the sole means of conveying information — text labels accompany all status indicators
  • aria-live regions announce dynamic content updates to screen readers
  • Dialog modals use role="dialog", aria-modal="true", and focus management

Your Rights

Because IEP Depot™ stores data only in your browser session, you can delete all data at any time by closing or refreshing the browser tab. No data deletion request to IEP Depot™ is needed — there is no server-side data to delete.

Disclaimer

IEP Depot™ is designed solely for IEP progress monitoring and compliance purposes. All goals, accommodations, and modifications entered into this tool are intended to mirror the student's official Individualized Education Program (IEP) document as written by the IEP team. IEP Depot™ does not create, alter, or replace any official IEP. Users are responsible for ensuring that all data entered accurately reflects the student's current, legally binding IEP. This tool is a reflection of the IEP — not a substitute for it.

US-Only License & Usage

IEP Depot™ is designed exclusively for use in United States schools and educational institutions operating under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA). Use of IEP Depot™ outside of the United States is not licensed, not supported, and may not comply with applicable laws in other jurisdictions. By using this tool, you confirm that you are operating within a US-based educational setting subject to IDEA and FERPA.

Vulnerability Disclosure Policy

IEP Depot™ is committed to the security of this tool and the privacy of all students it serves. If you discover a security vulnerability, bug, or potential privacy issue, please report it responsibly:

  • Email: hello@iepdepot.com — Subject line: "Security Disclosure"
  • Response time: We will acknowledge your report within 5 business days and provide an update within 14 business days.
  • Please do not publicly disclose any vulnerability until we have had the opportunity to investigate and issue a fix.
  • We appreciate responsible disclosure and will credit researchers who follow this process.

Version & Update Policy

Current Version: IEP Depot™ v5.0 — 2026

IEP Depot™ is updated on the following schedule:

  • Annual (August) — Federal IDEA/FERPA compliance review, state law updates, intervention program updates, accommodation category updates
  • Semi-annual (February) — Browser compatibility testing, bug fixes, and accessibility review
  • As needed — Critical security patches or federal/state special education law changes

To receive update notifications, register your copy at IEPDepot.com or email hello@iepdepot.com with subject "Register My Copy." Update notices are sent by email only — your tool does not auto-update.
